# The Hadith of Abu Dardaa'

This lesson revolves around the famous Hadith of [[Abu Dardaa']]

If possible, this Hadith should be memorised.

> [!Hadith] [[Abu Dardaa']] narrated:
> 
> فَإِنِّي سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ
> 
 ‏ "‏ مَنْ سَلَكَ طَرِيقًا يَطْلُبُ فِيهِ عِلْمًا سَلَكَ اللَّهُ بِهِ طَرِيقًا مِنْ طُرُقِ الْجَنَّةِ وَإِنَّ الْمَلاَئِكَةَ لَتَضَعُ أَجْنِحَتَهَا رِضًا لِطَالِبِ الْعِلْمِ وَإِنَّ الْعَالِمَ لَيَسْتَغْفِرُ لَهُ مَنْ فِي السَّمَوَاتِ وَمَنْ فِي الأَرْضِ وَالْحِيتَانُ فِي جَوْفِ الْمَاءِ وَإِنَّ فَضْلَ الْعَالِمِ عَلَى الْعَابِدِ كَفَضْلِ الْقَمَرِ لَيْلَةَ الْبَدْرِ عَلَى سَائِرِ الْكَوَاكِبِ وَإِنَّ الْعُلَمَاءَ وَرَثَةُ الأَنْبِيَاءِ وَإِنَّ الأَنْبِيَاءَ لَمْ يُوَرِّثُوا دِينَارًا وَلاَ دِرْهَمًا وَرَّثُوا الْعِلْمَ فَمَنْ أَخَذَهُ أَخَذَ بِحَظٍّ وَافِرٍ ‏"‏ ‏.‏
> 
> I heard the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) say: 
> 
> If anyone travels on a road in search of knowledge, Allah will cause him to travel on one of the roads of Paradise. The angels will lower their wings in their great pleasure with one who seeks knowledge, the inhabitants of the heavens and the Earth and the fish in the deep waters will ask forgiveness for the learned man. The superiority of the learned man over the devout is like that of the moon, on the night when it is full, over the rest of the stars. The learned are the heirs of the Prophets, and the Prophets leave neither dinar nor dirham, leaving only knowledge, and he who takes it takes an abundant portion.
> 
> {{ Sunan Abi Dawud 3641 }}

Note: Whenever knowledge is mentioned in revelation, what is meant is the knowledge of the Shari'ah.

**Benefits from this Hadith**:

1. By taking a path to knowledge, Allah will cause the person to travel on the road to Paradise/make it easy

	- Not being a scholar - just taking the path
	- This is contrast to other ahadith describing Jannah as difficult
	
2. The angels lower their wings in pleasure for the student of knowledge

	- For the student of knowledge, not the scholar
	- The Angels will not disobey Allah. So this implies the pleasure of Allah.
	
3. The inhabitants of the heavens & earth, and the fish in the sea seek forgiveness for the scholar (in some narrations: the student of knowledge)

4. The virtue of the scholar over the worshipper is like that of the moon over the stars

	- This is only for the scholar who acts upon their knowledge
	- Because they correct themselves and others.
	
5. The scholars are the heirs of the prophets

**The road to Jannah is described as easy in another narration**:

> [!Hadith] [[Abu Hurayrah]] narrated that the Prophet ﷺ said:
> 
> وَمَنْ سَلَكَ طَرِيقًا يَلْتَمِسُ فِيهِ عِلْمًا سَهَّلَ اللَّهُ لَهُ بِهِ طَرِيقًا إِلَى الْجَنَّةِ
> 
> Whoever sets out on a path in search of knowledge, Allah will make easy for him the path to Jannah
> 
> {{ Sahih Muslim 2699a }}
> 

**Jannah is not usually described as easy**:

> [!Hadith] [[Abu Hurayrah]] narrated that the Messenger ﷺ said:
> 
> حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو بَكْرِ بْنُ أَبِي النَّضْرِ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو النَّضْرِ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو عَقِيلٍ الثَّقَفِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو فَرْوَةَ، يَزِيدُ بْنُ سِنَانٍ التَّمِيمِيُّ حَدَّثَنِي بُكَيْرُ بْنُ فَيْرُوزَ، قَالَ سَمِعْتُ أَبَا هُرَيْرَةَ، يَقُولُ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم ‏ "‏ مَنْ خَافَ أَدْلَجَ وَمَنْ أَدْلَجَ بَلَغَ الْمَنْزِلَ أَلاَ ==إِنَّ سِلْعَةَ اللَّهِ غَالِيَةٌ== أَلاَ إِنَّ سِلْعَةَ اللَّهِ الْجَنَّةُ ‏"‏ ‏.‏ قَالَ أَبُو عِيسَى هَذَا حَدِيثٌ حَسَنٌ غَرِيبٌ لاَ نَعْرِفُهُ إِلاَّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي النَّضْرِ ‏.‏
> 
> "Whoever fears traveling at night - and whoever travels at night reaches his destination - **Allah provides him with the most precious of goods**, and indeed Allah's goods are but Paradise."

# Knowledge and action

 Aforementioned evidences already established this:

> [!Quran] [[Al-Fath]] ([[48:28]])
> 
> هُوَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَرْسَلَ رَسُولَهُۥ بِٱلْهُدَىٰ وَدِينِ ٱلْحَقِّ لِيُظْهِرَهُۥ عَلَى ٱلدِّينِ كُلِّهِۦ ۚ وَكَفَىٰ بِٱللَّهِ شَهِيدًۭا ٢٨
> 
> It is He who sent His Messenger with guidance and the religion of truth to manifest it over all religion. And sufficient is Allāh as Witness.

The ayah includes knowledge ("guidance") and action ("The religion of truth")

> [!Quran] [[Al-Fatiha]] ([[1-7]])
> 
> صِرَٰطَ ٱلَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ ==غَيْرِ ٱلْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا ٱلضَّآلِّينَ== ٧
> 
> The path of those upon whom You have bestowed favor, not of those who have earned [Your] anger or of those who are astray.

So, the "Sirat al-Mustaqim" is the path of knowledge and action combined

**A person of knowledge is measured by how much of the knowledge they have implemented:**

> [!Quran] [[Al-Zumar]] ([[39-9]]) 
> 
> أَمَّنْ هُوَ قَـٰنِتٌ ءَانَآءَ ٱلَّيْلِ سَاجِدًۭا وَقَآئِمًۭا يَحْذَرُ ٱلْـَٔاخِرَةَ وَيَرْجُوا۟ رَحْمَةَ رَبِّهِۦ ۗ قُلْ هَلْ يَسْتَوِى ٱلَّذِينَ يَعْلَمُونَ وَٱلَّذِينَ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ ۗ إِنَّمَا يَتَذَكَّرُ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَـٰبِ ٩
> 
> Is one who is devoutly obedient during periods of the night, prostrating and standing [in prayer], fearing the Hereafter and hoping for the mercy of his Lord, [like one who does not]? Say, "Are those who know equal to those who do not know?" Only they will remember [who are] people of understanding.

Benefits of the ayah:
- Demonstrates the virtue of knowledge
- Shows that knowledge is judged by actions

Allah described the people of knowledge by their actions, not the amount that they read, memorised and understood.

# What makes the Scholar superior to the worshipper:

So long as they are acting upon the knowledge (engaged in worship), they are:

1. Correcting & perfecting their worship
2. Correcting those around them - thus producing more worshippers
