# In the Qur'an

> [!Quran] [[Saba]] ([[34-37]]) 
> 
> وَمَآ أَمْوَٰلُكُمْ وَلَآ أَوْلَـٰدُكُم بِٱلَّتِى تُقَرِّبُكُمْ عِندَنَا زُلْفَىٰٓ ==إِلَّا مَنْ ءَامَنَ وَعَمِلَ صَـٰلِحًۭا== فَأُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ لَهُمْ جَزَآءُ ٱلضِّعْفِ ==بِمَا عَمِلُوا۟== وَهُمْ فِى ٱلْغُرُفَـٰتِ ءَامِنُونَ ٣٧
> 
> And it is not your wealth or your children that bring you nearer to Us in position, but it is [by being] one who has believed and done righteousness. For them there will be the double reward for what they did, and they will be in the upper chambers [of Paradise], safe [and secure].

Allah first mentioned "مَنْ ءَامَنَ وَعَمِلَ", and then when comes to mention it again simply mentions "بِمَا عَمِلُوا۟".

So the key here to this being an evidence is "بِمَا عَمِلُوا۟". It encompasses Iman with the heart as well as the righteous actions.

> [!Quran] [[Al-Baqarah]] ([[2-143]]) 
> 
> وَكَذَٰلِكَ جَعَلْنَـٰكُمْ أُمَّةًۭ وَسَطًۭا لِّتَكُونُوا۟ شُهَدَآءَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ وَيَكُونَ ٱلرَّسُولُ عَلَيْكُمْ شَهِيدًۭا ۗ وَمَا جَعَلْنَا ٱلْقِبْلَةَ ٱلَّتِى كُنتَ عَلَيْهَآ إِلَّا لِنَعْلَمَ مَن يَتَّبِعُ ٱلرَّسُولَ مِمَّن يَنقَلِبُ عَلَىٰ عَقِبَيْهِ ۚ وَإِن كَانَتْ لَكَبِيرَةً إِلَّا عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ هَدَى ٱللَّهُ ۗ ==وَمَا كَانَ ٱللَّهُ لِيُضِيعَ إِيمَـٰنَكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ بِٱلنَّاسِ لَرَءُوفٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ== ١٤٣
> 
> And thus We have made you a median [i.e., just] community that you will be witnesses over the people and the Messenger will be a witness over you. And We did not make the qiblah which you used to face except that We might make evident who would follow the Messenger from who would turn back on his heels. And indeed, it is difficult except for those whom Allāh has guided. **And never would Allāh have caused you to lose your faith [i.e., your previous prayers]. Indeed Allāh is, to the people, Kind and Merciful.**

This ayah was revealed about the changing of the Qiblah. All of the Scholars of Tafseer said that "Iman" here refers to "Salah":

> [!Quote] [[Imam Al-Halimi]] (Abu 'Abdillah al-Husayn ibn al-Hassan ibn Muhammed, d. 403 AH) said:
> 
> أَجْمَعَ الْمُفَسِّرُونَ عَلَى أَنَّهُ أَرَادَ صَلَاتَكُمْ إِلَى بَيْتِ الْمَقْدِسِ، فَثَبَتَ أَنَّ الصَّلَاةَ إِيمَانٌ، وَإِذَا ثَبَتَ ذَلِكَ، فَكُلُّ طَاعَةٍ إِيمَانٌ إِذْ لَمْ أَعْلَمْ فَارِقًا فِي هَذِهِ التَّسْمِيَةِ بَيْنَ الصَّلَاةِ وَسَائِرِ الْعِبَادَاتِ 
> 
> "The scholars of Tafsir unanimously agreed that it refers to your prayer towards Bayt al-Maqdis (Jerusalem), thus establishing that prayer is Iman. If this is established, then every act of obedience is Iman, as I do not know of any distinction made in this regard between prayer and other acts of worship.

That is, there is no linguistic reason to call prayer Iman, unless all acts of worships are Iman.

[[Imam Al-Halimi]] then quoted the following ayah as further evidence:

> [!Quran] [[Al-Anfal]] ([[8-2]]-4) 
> 
> إِنَّمَا ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ ٱلَّذِينَ إِذَا ذُكِرَ ٱللَّهُ وَجِلَتْ قُلُوبُهُمْ وَإِذَا تُلِيَتْ عَلَيْهِمْ ءَايَـٰتُهُۥ زَادَتْهُمْ إِيمَـٰنًۭا وَعَلَىٰ رَبِّهِمْ يَتَوَكَّلُونَ ٢
> 
> The believers are only those who, when Allāh is mentioned, their hearts become fearful, and when His verses are recited to them, it increases them in faith; and upon their Lord they rely -
> 
> ٱلَّذِينَ يُقِيمُونَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَـٰهُمْ يُنفِقُونَ ٣
> 
> The ones who establish prayer, and from what We have provided them, they spend.
> 
> أُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ حَقًّۭا ۚ لَّهُمْ دَرَجَـٰتٌ عِندَ رَبِّهِمْ وَمَغْفِرَةٌۭ وَرِزْقٌۭ كَرِيمٌۭ ٤
> 
> Those are the believers, truly. For them are degrees [of high position] with their Lord and forgiveness and noble provision.

Allah, in description of those who have Iman, only mentioned actions:
- Hearts tremble  (action of the heart)
- List to the ayaat they increase in Iman (action of the heart)
- Tawwakul (action of the heart)
- Perform the prayer
- Give charity

# In the Sunnah

> [!Hadith] Narrated [[Abdullah ibn 'Abbas]], that the Prophet ﷺ said to the delegation of 'Abdul Qais:
> 
> آمُرُكُمْ بِالإِيمَانِ بِاللَّهِ، وَهَلْ تَدْرُونَ مَا الإِيمَانُ بِاللَّهِ شَهَادَةُ أَنْ لاَ إِلَهَ إِلاَّ اللَّهُ، وَإِقَامُ الصَّلاَةِ، وَإِيتَاءُ الزَّكَاةِ، وَتُعْطُوا مِنَ الْمَغْنَمِ الْخُمُسَ
> 
> I order you to believe in Allah. Do you know what is meant by belief in Allah? It is to testify that none has the right to be worshipped except Allah, to offer prayers perfectly, to give Zakat, and to give Al-Khumus (one-fifth of the war booty) (in Allah's Cause).
> 
> {{ Sahih al-Bukhari 7556 }}

He ﷺ defined Iman with 4 actions.

> [!hadith] [[Abu Hurayrah]] narrated that the Prophet ﷺ said:
> 
> لاَ يَزْنِي الزَّانِي حِينَ يَزْنِي وَهُوَ مُؤْمِنٌ وَلاَ يَسْرِقُ حِينَ يَسْرِقُ وَهُوَ مُؤْمِنٌ وَلاَ يَشْرَبُ الْخَمْرَ حِينَ يَشْرَبُهَا وَهُوَ مُؤْمِنٌ وَالتَّوْبَةُ مَعْرُوضَةٌ بَعْدُ
> 
> A fornicator who fornicates is not a believer as long as he commits fornication, and no one who steals is a believer as long as he commits theft, and no one who drinks wine is a believer as long as he drinks it, and repentance may be accepted after that.
> 
> {{ Sahih Muslim 57f }}

# Statements of the Scholars

> [!Quote] [[Al-Awzaa'i]] said:
> 
> لَا يَسْتَقِيمُ الْإِيمَانُ إِلَّا بِالْقَوْلِ، وَلَا يَسْتَقِيمُ الْإِيمَانُ وَالْقَوْلُ إِلَّا بِالْعَمَلِ، وَلَا يَسْتَقِيمُ الْإِيمَانُ وَالْقَوْلُ وَالْعَمَلُ إِلَّا بِنِيَّةٍ مُوَافَقَةٍ لِلسُّنَّةِ ,
> 
> You cannot have Iman except with speech [i.e. the shahada], and Iman and that statement of faith except with action, and you cannot have Iman, a statement of faith, and action without an intention that is in accordance with the Sunnah.
> 
>  وَكَانَ مَنْ مَضَى مِنْ سَلَفِنَا لَا يُفَرِّقُونَ بَيْنَ الْإِيمَانِ وَالْعَمَلِ، وَالْعَمَلُ مِنَ الْإِيمَانِ , وَالْإِيمَانُ مِنَ الْعَمَلِ.
> 
> The predecessors among our Salaf did not differentiate between Iman and action; action is part of Iman, and Iman is part of action.
>  
>  وَإِنَّمَا الْإِيمَانُ اسْمٌ يَجْمَعُ هَذِهِ الْأَدْيَانَ اسْمُهَا , وَيُصَدِّقُهُ الْعَمَلُ ,
> 
>  Iman is a comprehensive term that encompasses these religious practices [all of the messages of the prophets] by name and is affirmed by action. 
>  
 فَمَنْ آمَنَ بِلِسَانِهِ وَعَرَفَ بِقَلْبِهِ , وَصَدَّقَ بِعَمَلِهِ , فَتِلْكَ الْعُرْوَةُ الْوُثْقَى الَّتِي لَا انْفِصَامَ لَهَا , وَمَنْ قَالَ بِلِسَانِهِ , وَلَمْ يَعْرِفْ بِقَلْبِهِ , وَلَمْ يُصَدِّقْهُ بِعَمَلِهِ , لَمْ يُقْبَلْ مِنْهُ , وَكَانَ فِي الْآخِرَةِ مِنَ الْخَاسِرِينَ
> 
>  So, whoever believes with his tongue, knows with his heart, and affirms it with his actions, that is the firm handhold that will never break. And whoever speaks with his tongue [says they believe], does not know with his heart, and does not affirm it with his actions, it will not be accepted from him, and he will be among the losers in the Hereafter [from the Munafiqin].

> [!quote] [[Imam Al-Shafi'i]] said:
> 
> ==كَانَ الْإِجْمَاعُ== مِنَ الصَّحَابَةِ وَالتَّابِعِينَ وَمَنْ بَعْدَهُمْ وَمَنْ أَدْرَكْنَاهُمْ يَقُولُونَ: ==الإِيمَانُ قَوْلٌ وَعَمَلٌ وَنِيَّةٌ== وَلَا يُجْزِئُ وَاحِدٌ مِنَ الثَّلَاثَةِ إِلَّا بِالْآخَرِ
> 
> "There was **consensus** among the Companions, the Followers, and those after them whom we met, saying: **Iman is speech, action, and intention,** and none of the three is sufficient without the others.

Intention is what is held in the heart - actions and beliefs of the hearts.

> [!quote] [[Imam al Bukhari]] said:
> 
> لَقِيتُ أَكْثَرَ مِنْ أَلْفِ رَجُلٍ مِنَ الْعُلَمَاءِ بِالْأَمْصَارِ، فَمَا رَأَيْتُ أَحَدًا مِنْهُمْ يَخْتَلِفُ فِي أَنَّ الْإِيمَانَ قَوْلٌ وَعَمَلٌ يَزِيدُ وَيَنْقُصُ.
> 
> I met more than a thousand men from the scholars of all the different cities I have not seen anyone disagree that Iman is made up of statements and actions and it increases and decreases

## Divisions of Iman:

Note, in the above quotes, Iman is divided slightly differently. But what is intended by all is the same:

**Dividing into five**:
1. Statement of the heart (belief/aqeedah)
2. Actions of the heart (e.g. loving, fearing, trusting)
3. Statements of the tongue (expressing belief through speech, primarily the shahada)
4. Actions of the tongue (dhikr, reciting Quran)
5. Actions of the limbs

**Dividing into four:**
1. Statement of the heart
2. Actions of the heart
3. Statements of the tongue
4. Actions of the limbs (inclusive of the tongue)

**Dividing into three**:
1. Statements | قول (tongue)
2. Actions | عمل (limbs)
3. Intentions | نية (heart)
e.g. the statement of [[Imam Al-Shafi'i]]

**Dividing into two**:
- Statements | قول
	- Statements if the heart (belief)
	- Statements of the tongue (e.g. Shahada)
- Action | عمل
	- Actions of the heart
	- Actions of the tongue (e.g. dhikr)
	- Action of the limbs
e.g. the statement of [[Imam al Bukhari]]

# Iman vs 'Aqeedah

When Iman is used specifically as a synonym of 'Aqeedah, it refers specifically to the statement of the beliefs of the heart (قول القلب).

But in the general sense, Iman is broader than 'Aqeedah, as statements of the heart are only one subset of Iman.

> [!Quote] [[Ibn Taymiyyah]] said:
> 
> "وَأَمَّا سَائِرُ الْفُقَهَاءِ مِنْ أَهْلِ الرَّأْيِ وَالْآثَارِ بِالْحِجَازِ وَالْعِرَاقِ وَالشَّامِ وَمِصْرَ مِنْهُمْ مَالِكُ بْنُ أَنَسٍ وَاللَّيْثُ بْنُ سَعْدٍ وَسُفْيَانُ الثَّوْرِيُّ وَالْأَوْزَاعِيُّ وَالشَّافِعِيُّ وَأَحْمَدُ بْنُ حَنْبَلٍ وَإِسْحَاقُ بْنُ رَاهَوَيْهِ وَأَبُو عُبَيْدِ الْقَاسِمُ بْنُ سَلامٍ وَدَاوُدُ بْنُ عِليٍّ وَأَبُو جَعْفَرٍ الطَّبَرِيُّ وَمَنْ سَلَكَ سَبِيلَهُمْ
> 
> As for the rest of the jurists from the people of opinion and Athar in Hijaz, Iraq, Sham, and Egypt - among them Malik ibn Anas, Al-Layth ibn Sa'd, Sufyan Al-Thawri, Al-Awza'i, Al-Shafi'i, Ahmad ibn Hanbal, Ishaq ibn Rahwayh, Abu Ubayd Al-Qasim ibn Salam, Dawud ibn Ali, and Abu Ja'far Al-Tabari, and those who followed their path - 
> 
>  قَالُوا: الإِيمَانُ قَوْلٌ وَعَمَلٌ، قَوْلٌ بِاللِّسَانِ وَهُوَ الإِقْرَار، وَاعْتِقَادٌ بِالقَلْبِ وَعَمَلٌ بِالجَوَارِحِ مَعَ الإِخْلَاصِ بِالنِّيَّةِ الصَّادِقَةِ.
> 
> they said: **Iman is speech and action, speech with the tongue which is affirmation, belief in the heart, and action with the limbs along with sincerity in intention**. 
>  
 وَقَالُوا: كُلُّ مَا يُطَاعُ اللَّهُ بِهِ مِنْ فَرِيضَةٍ وَنَافِلَةٍ فَهُوَ مِنَ الإِيمَانِ، وَقَالُوا: وَالإِيمَانُ يَزِيدُ بِالطَّاعَاتِ وَيَنْقُصُ بِالْمَعَاصِي."
> 
> And they said: **Everything by which Allah is obeyed, whether obligatory or voluntary, is part of Iman, and they said: Iman increases with obedience and decreases with sins**."

> [!quote] [[Ibn Taymiyyah]] also said
> 
> وَأَجْمَعَ السَّلَفُ أَنَّ الْإِيمَانَ قَوْلٌ وَعَمَلٌ يَزِيدُ وَيَنْقُصُ وَمَعْنَى ذَلِكَ أَنَّهُ قَوْلُ الْقَلْبِ وَعَمَلُ الْقَلْبِ ثُمَّ قَوْلُ اللِّسَانِ وَعَمَلُ الْجَوَارِحِ. فَأَمَّا قَوْلُ الْقَلْبِ فَهُوَ التَّصْدِيقُ الْجَازِمُ بِاَللَّهِ وَمَلَائِكَتِهِ وَكُتُبِهِ وَرُسُلِهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الْآخِرِ وَيَدْخُلُ فِيهِ الْإِيمَانُ بِكُلِّ مَا جَاءَ بِهِ الرَّسُولُ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ
> 
> "The predecessors unanimously agreed that Iman is speech and action, increasing and decreasing. This means that it consists of the statement of the heart and the action of the heart, then the statement of the tongue and the action of the limbs. The statement of the heart is the firm belief in Allah, His angels, His books, His messengers, and the Last Day, and it includes believing in everything that the Messenger (peace be upon him) brought."

Here:
- He divided Iman into 4
- He defined the statement of the heart as 'Aqeedah (6 pillars of Iman, everything the prophet told you to believe)

> [!question] Why did [[Ibn Taymiyyah]] say Iman means "tasdeeq", despite the previous discussion?
> 
> Response: he said, =="التَّصْدِيقُ الْجَازِمُ"== which is firm/resolute belief which in the meaning og Iqrar (affirming with conviction)

> [!Quote] [[Haafidh al-Hakami]] (d. 1377 AH)
> 
> فَهَذِهِ أَرْبَعَةُ أَشْيَاءَ جَامِعَةٌ لِأُمُورِ دِينِ الْإِسْلَامِ:
> 
>  الْأَوَّلُ: قَوْلُ الْقَلْبِ وَهُوَ تَصْدِيقُهُ وَإِيقَانُهُ.
>  الثَّانِي: قَوْلُ اللِّسَانِ وَهُوَ النُّطْقُ بِالشَّهَادَتَيْنِ 
>  
>  الثَّالِثُ: عَمَلُ الْقَلْبِ, وَهُوَ النِّيَّةُ وَالْإِخْلَاصُ وَالْمَحَبَّةُ وَالِانْقِيَادُ وَالْإِقْبَالُ عَلَى اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ وَالتَّوَكُّلُ عَلَيْهِ وَلَوَازِمُ ذَلِكَ وَتَوَابِعُهُ.
>  
>  الرَّابِعُ: عَمَلُ اللِّسَانِ وَالْجَوَارِحِ, فَعَمَلُ اللِّسَانِ مَا لَا يُؤَدَّى إِلَّا بِهِ كَتِلَاوَةِ الْقُرْآنِ وَسَائِرِ الْأَذْكَارِ مِنَ التَّسْبِيحِ وَالتَّحْمِيدِ وَالتَّهْلِيلِ وَالتَّكْبِيرِ وَالدُّعَاءِ وَالِاسْتِغْفَارِ وَغَيْرِ ذَلِكَ, وعمل الجوارح ما لَا يُؤَدَّى إِلَّا بِهَا مِثْلُ الْقِيَامِ وَالرُّكُوعِ وَالسُّجُودِ وَالْمَشْيِ فِي مَرْضَاةِ اللَّهِ كَنَقْلِ الخطا إلى المساجد وإلى الْحَجِّ وَالْجِهَادِ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ وَالْأَمْرِ بِالْمَعْرُوفِ وَالنَّهْيِ عَنِ الْمُنْكَرِ, وَغَيْرِ ذَلِكَ مِمَّا يَشْمَلُهُ حَدِيثُ
> 
> "These are four comprehensive aspects of the religion of Islam:
> 
> First: The statement of the heart, which is its belief and certainty.
> 
> Second: The statement of the tongue, which is the utterance of the two testimonies of faith and affirm whatever is entailed by it.
> 
> Third: The action of the heart, which includes intention, sincerity, love, submission, turning towards Allah Almighty, reliance on Him, and the necessary and related aspects of this.
> 
> Fourth: The action of the tongue and the limbs. The action of the tongue includes what cannot be performed except by it, such as reciting the Quran and other forms of dhikr (remembrance of Allah), including tasbeeh (glorifying Allah), tahmeed (praising Allah), tahleel (declaring the oneness of Allah), takbeer (exalting Allah), supplication, and seeking forgiveness. The action of the limbs includes what cannot be performed except by them, such as standing, bowing, prostration, and walking in obedience to Allah, like taking steps towards the mosques, pilgrimage, and jihad in the way of Allah Almighty, enjoining good, forbidding evil, and other actions encompassed by hadith."
